I’m Kirk Fuson, a visual artist and storyteller building Echo Line—a living, serialized experience that blends narrative, art, and atmosphere. My north star is simple: cyberpunk grit with a human heart.

If you want to see how this project started visually, I’ve archived early cover explorations and concept pieces here: Geisha 2.0 — Book Cover Art (early works) .

Influences & mood

The writing pulls from a stack of heroes—William Gibson, Neal Stephenson, Ray Bradbury, and Jack London—filtered through films like Blade Runner, Three Days of the Condor, and Heat. That mix of atmospheric paranoia, quiet professionalism, and rough-edged hope is the texture I’m chasing in Echo Line.

Follow the art

I post process shots, covers, and in-world ephemera on Instagram: @FuManKirk.

What to expect here

  • Chapters released in sequence, each with notes, lore, and sketches.
  • Standalone extras (secret pages, terminals, ephemera) woven into the world.
  • Iterative updates as the story grows and the city reveals new angles.
